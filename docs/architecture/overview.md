# System Architecture

Enklava operates on a **Three-Layer Stack** — separating immutable assets from scalable operations to maximize security, tax efficiency, and operational continuity.

## Ecosystem Governance Model

The three layers serve distinct and complementary functions in the ecosystem:

| Layer | Entity | Primary Function | Ecosystem Role |
|-------|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **A** | Fundacja Enklava | Land holding, mission stewardship | Mission guardian |
| **B** | Enklava Sp. z o.o. | Reference architecture, IP, R&D | Product owner |
| **C** | Kinterra | Partner platform, global scale | **Ecosystem orchestrator** |

This separation ensures that product development (Enklava) remains focused on technical excellence while ecosystem growth (Kinterra) can scale independently.

The layers are interdependent but autonomous: Fundacja cannot override Enklava's technical decisions; Enklava cannot dictate Kinterra's partner relationships; Kinterra cannot compromise Fundacja's mission commitments.

## Why This Structure?

### The Infinite Game Problem

Most startups are designed to exit. Founders build, investors extract, company gets acquired or dies. The assets get flipped. The mission gets abandoned.

**Enklava is designed to never exit.**

### The Solution: Layered Sovereignty

| Risk | Protection |
|------|------------|
| Land speculation | Layer A (Fundacja) legally cannot sell |
| Hostile acquisition | Layer B (Sp. z o.o.) controlled by multi-sig, not single founder |
| Partner departure | Layer C partners can leave without killing the protocol |
| Founder death | Succession protocol with Prokura + multi-sig |

### The Key Insight

**Layer A protects the mission. Layer B executes the mission. Layer C scales the mission.**

Even if Layer C partners exit, Layer B continues. Even if Layer B company fails, Layer A still holds the land. The protocol survives any single point of failure.
